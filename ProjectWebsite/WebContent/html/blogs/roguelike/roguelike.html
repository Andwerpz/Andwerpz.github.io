About Me

My second platformer project was great and all, but I felt like the idea of making a 2D bullet hell game with gravity was inherently flawed, since the bullet hell aspect didn't mesh at all with the gravity meschanic. There was probably a way to solve this in an interesting and creative way, but after a few months, I had gotten burnt out. The foundational structure of the game also wasn't too great; adding weapons and enemies was a pain, as well as importing the textures for said weapons and enemies.

Long story short, I had learned alot while working on Platformer 2, and I decided it would be a good idea to start with a clean slate to try to put some things I learned to use. As I still wanted to make a bullet hell type game, I axed gravity (Sorry, Newton), and decided to make a top down game instead, aptly named: Roguelike.


Textures

One of the major things that bugged me while working on Platformer 2, was the look of the game, namely the textures. Although the ground in Platformer 2 looked ok, they couldn't really stand on their own without lighting, and for the player, I completely gave up and just drew a placeholder rectangle over the environment hitbox.
